Sealed-Bid Auctions

Overview

Watch LLM agents compete in sealed-bid auctions with independent private values. Each round, bidders receive a random private value and must decide how much to bid without knowing the opponent's value or bid.

Auction Formats

First-Price Highest bidder wins and pays their own bid. Strategic bid shading is optimal.
Second-Price (Vickrey) Highest bidder wins but pays the second-highest bid. Truthful bidding (bid = value) is a dominant strategy.

Theoretical Equilibria

With 2 bidders and uniform values on [0, max]:

First-Price Equilibrium: bid = value / 2
Second-Price Equilibrium: bid = value (dominant)

The "bid comparison" shows how the actual bid compares to the theoretical equilibrium bid.

Key Metrics

Efficiency % of rounds where the highest-value bidder won
Bid Shading How much below value the bidder bids on average
Revenue Total amount paid across all auctions

Strategic Insights

In first-price auctions, bidding your true value guarantees zero profit if you win. The optimal strategy involves "bid shading" - bidding below your value to maintain profit margin while still having a reasonable chance to win.

In second-price auctions, your bid only affects whether you win, not what you pay. Bidding truthfully is always optimal regardless of opponent behavior.
